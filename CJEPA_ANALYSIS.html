01 · Where in the pipeline

Five real bugs, spread across four pipeline stages

Each MPC eval round found and fixed a genuine bug — never a phantom. 03 · Stage-by-stage validation, 2026-07-06

The encoder is fine. The signal is real but unreliable.

Rather than keep guessing at wiring bugs, this session validated two pipeline stages independently, bypassing CEM entirely. First finding: the frozen representation is not the bottleneck. Second: the predictor does carry real action-sensitivity on average, but its reliability swings too widely per episode for CEM to exploit.

04 · Next investigation, queued

What separates a reliable state from an unreliable one?

17.2% of episodes are excellent (real action beats ≥90% of random candidates); 10.9% are actively inverted. That spread looks like state-dependent reliability CEM can't detect in advance — it plans one state at a time and can't average across episodes the way this diagnostic did. The plan: correlate per-episode covariates against the percentile score and let the result choose the fix.
